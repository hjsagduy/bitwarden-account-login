What Is a Bitwarden Account?
----------------------------

A Bitwarden account securely stores your digital credentials in an encrypted vault. Instead of remembering dozens of passwords, you only need your Master Password to unlock your vault.

With a Bitwarden account, you can:

* Store unlimited passwords and passkeys.
* Save secure notes and personal information.
* Store payment cards and identities.
* Generate strong, unique passwords.
* Sync your vault across multiple devices.
* Securely share credentials with trusted users.
* Monitor password strength and improve account security.

How to Perform a Bitwarden Account Login
----------------------------------------

Follow these steps to access your account:

1. Open the Bitwarden Web Vault, desktop application, browser extension, or mobile app.
2. Enter the email address associated with your Bitwarden account.
3. Type your Master Password.
4. Complete two-factor authentication (2FA) if it is enabled.
5. After successful authentication, your encrypted vault will be unlocked.

Common Bitwarden Account Login Issues
-------------------------------------

Incorrect Master Password
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

The most common login issue is entering an incorrect Master Password.

Before trying again:

* Verify your keyboard layout.
* Check that Caps Lock is off.
* Ensure there are no typing mistakes.

Two-Factor Authentication Problems
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

If your verification code isn't accepted:

* Open your authenticator application.
* Confirm your device's date and time are correct.
* Try using a recovery code if one was saved during setup.

Browser Extension Problems
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

If the Bitwarden extension does not respond:

#. Refresh your browser.
#. Update the extension.
#. Restart your browser.
#. Reinstall the extension if needed.

Network Connection Issues
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

Bitwarden requires an active internet connection to authenticate your account and synchronize changes.

If login fails:

* Check your Wi-Fi or mobile data.
* Try another network.
* Disable any VPN temporarily if it is causing connection issues.

Unable to Access Your Vault
---------------------------

If your vault does not unlock after signing in:

* Verify that you are using the correct email address.
* Ensure your Master Password is correct.
* Confirm that your account has not been locked due to repeated failed attempts.
* Restart the application and try again.

-----------------------------------------

Improve your account security by following these recommendations:

* Create a long, unique Master Password.
* Enable two-factor authentication.

* Update the Bitwarden app and browser extensions regularly.
* Avoid logging in from public or shared computers.
* Lock your vault when your device is unattended.
* Regularly review your saved passwords and replace weak or reused credentials.

Frequently Asked Questions
--------------------------

Can I log in to Bitwarden on multiple devices?
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

Yes. Your encrypted vault can be synchronized across desktop computers, mobile devices, and supported web browsers.

Does Bitwarden store my Master Password?
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

No. Bitwarden follows a zero-knowledge security model, meaning your Master Password is never stored in plain text and cannot be recovered by the service.

Can I use biometric authentication?
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

Yes. Many Android, iPhone, Windows, and macOS devices support fingerprint recognition, Face ID, or Windows Hello after the initial account login.

What should I do if I forget my Master Password?
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

For security reasons, Bitwarden cannot retrieve your Master Password. If you have configured account recovery options or securely stored your emergency information, those may help depending on your setup. Otherwise, you may need to reset your account according to Bitwarden's recovery process.

How can I improve my account security?
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

Use a unique Master Password, enable two-factor authentication, keep your applications updated, and regularly review your vault for weak or reused passwords.
